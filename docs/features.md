Here is a comprehensive list of features from Football Manager 2026, broken down into categories for clarity.

### 🎯 Core Management & Career
- **Global Database**: Manages one of the largest databases in gaming, with 58 men's and 11 women's nations, including full women's football integration for the first time.
- **Manager Customization**: Create detailed manager profiles, including appearance, coaching badges, playing history, and personality.
- **Career Start Options**: Offers Quick Start for immediate play or Advanced Setup to customize every league, database, and start date detail.
- **Delegation**: Allows delegating many management tasks (youth teams, transfers, training) to assistant staff.
- **Save Compatibility**: Supports loading saved careers from FM23 and FM24 on same platform.

### ⚙️ Tactical & Match Systems
- **New Tactical System**: Introduces separate formations for possession and out-of-possession phases, a major tactical evolution.
- **Revamped Player Roles**: Features new roles (e.g., Playmaking Wing-Back) and removed outdated ones (e.g., Trequartista) to support new tactics.
- **Tactics Visualiser**: Dynamic tool previews how tactics, formations, and roles perform on the pitch in different phases.
- **Match Engine Upgrades**: Offers smarter passing (line-breaking passes), dribbling (feints), attacking (cutbacks), defending (pressing shapes), and goalkeeping.

### 👥 Team & Player Management
- **Dynamic Squad Building**: Includes new tools like the Squad Planner and a Provisional Squad system for managing larger player pools.
- **In-Match Shouts**: Feature returned by popular demand, allowing commands to players from the dugout.

### 🌍 International Management
- **International Management**: Fully integrated international management, including women's national teams for the first time.
- **Dedicated World Cup Mode**: Features a quick-start mode for the fully licensed, official 2026 FIFA World Cup.
- **National Team Tools**: Includes a National Shortlist for players, scouting focuses on dual nationals, and management of player expectations.

### 💼 Transfer & Scouting
- **Revamped Scouting System**: Features National Shortlist tabs, Player Discovery views, and scouting filters for both immediate and long-term needs.
- **New Transfer Tools**: Incorporates real-world platform TransferRoom with features like "Adverts" to broadcast needs and "Pitch Opportunities" to track rivals.
- **Scouting & Transfers UI Overhaul**: Streamlined workflows for loan screens, player searches, and transfer offers.

### 📈 Data, UI & Navigation
- **Redesigned UI**: Extensive overhaul of the UI for more intuitive navigation.
- **FMPedia**: New in-game knowledge database with explanations and links to game systems.
- **Data Hub Integration**: Enhanced data analytics for international and club management, including match preparation training focuses.
- **Enhanced Navigation**: Features bookmarks for quick access, an overhauled universal search bar, and a "back" navigation option.

### 🏆 Competitions & Licensing
- **FIFA World Cup 2026 License**: First-ever official FIFA licensing agreement in the series, featuring authentic broadcast graphics, kits, and match ball.
- **Major League Licenses**: Includes fully licensed Premier League, WSL, NWSL, and competitions across 11 nations.
- **Women's Football Debut**: Fully integrated women's club and national team football with authentic leagues and competitions.

### 🖥️ Technology & Performance
- **Unity Engine**: The franchise's first full switch to the Unity engine for major graphical upgrades, improved ball physics, and smoother animations.
- **Performance Optimizations**: Significantly reduced loading times and smoother menu transitions, even on average laptops.
- **Enhanced Matchday Experience**: Features dynamic highlights, better camera angles, improved stadium/crowd environments, and realistic lighting.
- **Graphics and Animations**: Significant improvements to player movement, realistic take-ons, and volumetric animations from motion capture.

### ➕ Quality of Life & Miscellaneous
- **Community & Mod Support**: Extensive support for user-created tactics, database add-ons, and graphic packs.
- **Cross-Save Support**: Feature for Mobile series allowing save game compatibility across different devices.
- **Content Updates**: Post-launch content updates delivering new features and licensed content free of charge.
- **Customization**: More granular control over game settings like database depth, transfer rules, and start dates.
- **Controller Support**: Revamped and improved controller support for PC, consoles, and handhelds.
- **Set-Piece Coaches & Training**: Focuses on specialized coaching for set-pieces and match preparation.
- **Provisional Squad & Player Recovery**: System for tournament squad management and adjusted player recovery.
- **UI Polish & Bug Fixes**: Continuous updates providing extensive bug fixes, stability improvements, and UI refinements.
- **Free Play Periods**: Events allowing players to try the full game for free for a limited time.
- **Dual Nationality Management**: Scouting focuses specifically to help find dual nationals eligible for your country.
- **International Expectations**: Interface showing what role a player expects on the national team.
- **License Authenticity**: Officially licensed FIFA World Cup 2026 and other major leagues for an authentic experience.
- **Tactical Integration**: Direct links from the tactics screen to the Data Hub for data-driven decision making.

### 🎮 Feature Parity for Your TUI Game
- **Graphical Features**: Features heavily reliant on 3D graphics (match engine, lighting, animations) are irrelevant for a TUI game.
- **Simplicity Priority**: Your game should focus on accurate, deep simulation of management systems (tactics, transfers, international management) without graphical complexity.
- **Data-Heavy Core**: Your TUI game should mirror FM26's data-driven approach through table-based displays for player attributes, financial reports, and match statistics.
- **System Optimization**: Optimizing the game for a "potato PC" is crucial, mirroring FM26's focus on performance improvements for lower-end machines.
